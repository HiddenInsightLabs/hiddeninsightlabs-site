The Problem with Internal Testing

Your Team Is Too Good
at Knowing Your Product.

That sounds like a strength. In testing, it's a liability. The more your team understands how something works, the less they can experience it as a stranger would. They skip the confusion. They fill in the gaps. They know which button to press. Your users don't.

Tester Network — Global. Diverse. Robust.

Real devices, real conditions — mobile, tablet, desktop, legacy hardware, AR-capable hardware. The long tail your QA lab doesn't cover.

No shared assumptions — our analysts don't know your roadmap, your intent, or your workarounds. They only know what's in front of them. That's the point.

Structured signal, not chaos — every analyst follows a defined evaluation protocol. What comes back isn't a list of complaints. It's a prioritised, actionable report.

How It Works

Simple to Start.
Serious When It Matters.

STEP 01

Tell Us What You're Worried About

You know your product better than anyone. You also know which parts make you nervous. We start there — a focused conversation about your release, your risk areas, and what kind of users we should be recruiting to test it.

STEP 02

We Put Perfect or Imperfect People into Tests on Your Product

We recruit from our analyst network — matching device profile, experience level, and region to your actual target audience. They use your software the way real users do: imperfectly, unpredictably, and without reading the manual, unless you need the opposite.

STEP 03

You Get a Report You Can Actually Use

Not a spreadsheet of complaints. A structured, prioritised insight report — friction points ranked by severity, supported by tester quotes, completion data, and specific recommendations. Your developers know exactly what to fix first.
